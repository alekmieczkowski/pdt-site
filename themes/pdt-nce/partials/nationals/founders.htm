Founding Fathers
Robert Morrison

Robert Morrison was born on March 15, 1822 on a farm in Greene County, Pennsylvania. He organized churches and missions, and did domestic mission work in Missouri, as well as became a principal at Waterford Academy in Ohio. His philosophy was “To do what ought to be done but what would not have been done unless I did it, I thought to be my duty.

Robert T. Drake

Robert Thompson Drake was born in Clark County, Ohio on March 6, 1822. A product of Miami University’s Class of 1850, Drake taught mathematics and natural in Anderson’s Female Seminary. He devoted much of his life to preaching all throughout the Midwest.

John W. Lindley

Mr. Lindley was a third cousin of Robert Morrison. He was also born in Ohio, in Knox County. He received his A.B. degree from Miami in 1850 and his M.A. degree in 1853. He attended many of the Fraternity Conventions, the most prominent ones being the Semi-Centennial celebration in 1898 and the General Convention in New York in 1902. He was the last surviving founder, passing away at the age of 81.

John M. Wilson

John McMillian Wilson was born on September 10, 1825 in Union County, Indiana. He attended Xenia Academy, and entered Miami University in 1846. He received an A.B. degree in 1849 and an M.A. degree in 1852. Later in his life he was commissioned as a recruiting officer in both Ohio and Indiana. Undergraduate students knew him as “Pop” Wilson.

Andrew W. Rogers

Andrew Watts Rogers attended the prep school at Miami University in 1846 and graduated with a degree in 1851 and earning a M.A. degree in 1854. He, like many of the Founding Fathers, was a teacher at an academy. He also served as lieutenant colonel during the Civil War.

Ardivan W. Rodgers

Ardivan Walker Rodgers was born in Miami County near Piqua, Ohio, on October, 20,1824. His grandpa was a captain in the war of 1812. He received two degrees at the seminary at Oxford. Unfortunately, he died from typhoid fever at the age of 32 and was the first founder to pass away.
